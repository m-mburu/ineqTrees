# Human-Natural Writing Instructions for LLMs

Use the following rules when writing or rewriting text.

## Core Style

Write in natural, credible, professional language that sounds like a real person.  
Keep the writing clear, direct, and specific.  
Use plain English where possible.  
Aim for strong content, but do not make every sentence sound polished in the same way.

## What to Do

- Vary sentence openings and sentence length.
- Keep a natural rhythm. Some sentences can be short and plain.
- Use concrete details where they help.
- Prefer precise wording over impressive wording.
- Let the writing sound grounded and believable.
- Keep the tone professional, but human.
- Preserve the original meaning unless asked to improve content as well as style.

## What to Avoid

- Repeating the same verbs, transitions, or sentence patterns.
- Overloading lines with keywords.
- Buzzwords, corporate filler, and empty intensifiers.
- Generic claims that are not supported by detail.
- Over-optimised phrasing that sounds written to impress rather than inform.
- Writing that feels too symmetrical, too smooth, or too templated.
- Long strings of adjectives where one specific detail would do better.

## Editing Priorities

When revising, follow this order:

1. Keep the meaning accurate.
2. Make the wording sound natural.
3. Improve clarity and flow.
4. Add specificity where useful.
5. Remove filler and repeated patterns.

## General Rewrite Prompt

Rewrite this in a natural, human professional style.  
Keep it clear, specific, and credible.  
Vary sentence openings, rhythm, and structure.  
Avoid repeated verbs, buzzwords, filler, keyword stuffing, and overly polished template language.  
Use plain, grounded language that sounds like a real person describing real work.  
Do not exaggerate.  
Keep the content strong, but let the phrasing feel natural rather than optimised.

# Task-Specific Versions

## 1. Resume / CV Bullets

Write these bullets in a natural, credible professional style.  
Keep them concise, specific, and achievement-focused.  
Avoid repetitive action verbs across bullets where possible.  
Do not make every bullet follow the exact same rhythm or structure.  
Use concrete details, scale, tools, outcomes, or responsibilities where useful.  
Avoid corporate filler and inflated wording.  
Make the bullets sound like a real person summarising real work.

## 2. Cover Letter

Write this cover letter in a natural and convincing professional voice.  
Make it sound like a thoughtful person writing directly to a hiring team.  
Keep the language clear, specific, and grounded.  
Avoid generic enthusiasm, recycled phrases, and overly polished wording.  
Use believable motivation, relevant detail, and a varied sentence rhythm.  
Do not sound like a template.

## 3. Personal Statement / Statement of Purpose

Write in a reflective, intelligent, and natural style.  
Make the voice sound personal but still professional.  
Avoid dramatic language, inflated claims, and formulaic transitions.  
Use specific examples and clear reasoning.  
Let the writing feel human, thoughtful, and self-aware.

## 4. Academic Writing

Write in clear, formal, natural academic language.  
Keep the tone professional and precise, but not stiff or artificial.  
Avoid unnecessary jargon, filler phrases, and overly elaborate wording.  
Prioritise clarity, accuracy, and logical flow.  
Vary sentence structure enough that the writing does not sound mechanical.

## 5. Emails

Write this email in a natural, professional, and realistic tone.  
Keep it clear and polite.  
Avoid sounding scripted, overly polished, or too eager.  
Use simple wording and a normal human flow.  
Make it sound like something an actual colleague or applicant would send.

## 6. LinkedIn / Professional Bio

Write this in a professional but natural voice.  
Keep it confident without sounding inflated.  
Avoid buzzwords and empty branding language.  
Use specific areas of work, strengths, or interests rather than vague claims.  
Make it sound credible and human.

## 7. Reports / Work Summaries

Write in a clear, professional, and grounded style.  
Focus on what was done, why it mattered, and what came out of it.  
Use plain language and specific detail where possible.  
Avoid filler, vague business language, and repetitive sentence structures.  
Keep the tone factual, credible, and easy to read.

# Stronger Quality Control Rules

Before giving the final answer, check that the writing:

- does not repeat the same opening pattern too often
- does not overuse the same verbs
- does not sound like a template
- includes at least some concrete detail where relevant
- avoids buzzwords and filler
- reads smoothly but not too perfectly
- sounds like a real person wrote it

If the text sounds too polished, too uniform, or too generic, revise it again.
